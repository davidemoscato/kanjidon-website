Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026 | Version 3.1

Plain-language summary

Kanjidon stores the information needed to provide your account, learning progress, purchases, community features, support, and app security. Some activity is shown in the Live community feed to other signed-in Kanjidon users. We do not publish your email address, payment details, IP address, private note text, or coin balance in Live.

We do not sell personal data. You can delete your account in the app, manage the available privacy and notification settings, block users, and contact us to exercise your data-protection rights or object to Live processing.

1. Controller and scope

The controller is Davide Moscato, VAT ID IT01691020083, Italy. Email: info@kanjidon.com.

This policy applies to the Kanjidon mobile application, the Kanjidon website, and the connected support, authentication, community, notification, and purchase services.

2. Who may use Kanjidon

Kanjidon is not intended for children under 13. If local law requires parental authorization for consent-based online services, the child must have that authorization. In Italy, a child under 14 cannot independently provide consent for this type of processing.

We do not currently ask every user for a date of birth. If we learn that an account is being used by a child without the authorization required by law, we may restrict the social features or delete the relevant personal data. Parents and guardians may contact us at info@kanjidon.com.

3. Data we process

3.1 Account and profile

  • Account identifier, email address, authentication provider, and account timestamps.
  • Display name, avatar, border, language, country code, and profile preferences.
  • Privacy, notification, audio, appearance, and gameplay settings.

3.2 Learning and gameplay

  • Cards, packs, collections, decks, deck names, private notes, showcases, stamps, and rewards.
  • Quiz answers, accuracy, response times, SRS reviews, lessons, stories, mock exams, progress, streaks, missions, ranks, and leaderboards.
  • Battle participation, opponents, scores, matchmaking information, and anti-cheat or fairness signals.

3.3 Community and safety

  • Friend codes, requests, friendships, blocks, Ganbatte interactions, showcase interactions, and community activity.
  • User reports: authenticated reporter identity, reported user, reason, optional description, timestamps, status, and moderation records where available.
  • Support messages and the information you choose to include in them.

3.4 Purchases and subscriptions

  • Product, transaction and receipt identifiers, purchase history, trial information, and subscription status.
  • Apple and Google process payment details. Kanjidon does not receive your full payment-card number.

3.5 Device, notifications, diagnostics, and analytics

  • App version, platform, device and operating-system information, security logs, failures, and performance information.
  • Firebase installation or app-instance identifiers, a pseudonymous account identifier after sign-in, screens and app interactions, gameplay events, and purchase events used for analytics.
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging token, language, notification preferences, and delivery status.

Analytics data is pseudonymous, not necessarily anonymous. Kanjidon enables Firebase Analytics in release builds. We use it to understand feature use, stability, and purchase flows.

3.6 Microphone and speech recognition

When you start a Shadowing exercise, Kanjidon uses the microphone and the speech-recognition service provided by your device platform to convert a short voice sample into text. Depending on the device, downloaded language models, and platform settings, recognition may happen on the device or the voice sample may be sent to the platform provider for recognition.

Microphone use is optional and starts only after you tap the recording control and grant permission. Kanjidon does not upload or store the recording or recognized text in its own backend; the transient result is used in memory to score the current exercise. The platform provider may process the voice sample under its own terms and device privacy settings.

3.7 Country detection and network data

On first sign-in, the server may process the connection IP address and send it to country.is to derive a two-letter country code. Kanjidon stores the country code, not the IP address used for that lookup. Infrastructure providers may independently process IP addresses in normal security and access logs.

4. Live community feed

Live is an activity feed available to signed-in Kanjidon users. Personal Live events are generated automatically from eligible activity; they are not manually written posts.

4.1 What other signed-in users may see

  • Your display name, avatar, border, country flag, and event time.
  • Learning and collection milestones, quizzes, lessons, stories, reviews, streaks, missions, cards, packs, stamps, ranks, and achievements.
  • Deck creation and the deck name, but not the private contents of your notes.
  • Battle mode, opponent display information, and scores.
  • Showcase, store-review, and Kanjidon Pass subscription events.

4.2 What Live does not expose

  • Your email address, authentication credentials, or exact IP address.
  • Full payment details, purchase receipts, or coin balance.
  • The text of private card notes.

4.3 Audience, ranking, and aggregates

Live personal events may be seen by signed-in users worldwide. The Live feed is not available to anonymous app users. The feed can prioritize and diversify events using recency, event type, and repeated-author limits. Country and community headlines use aggregated activity and are emitted only when minimum participation thresholds are met.

4.4 Visibility and objection

The standard Live feed returns events from the previous 30 days. Underlying event records may remain while the relevant account is active for feed integrity, deduplication, safety, and dispute handling. Events attributed to a profile are deleted when that profile is deleted; limited information contained in an event attributed to another user may remain until that event is deleted, subject to applicable legal rights.

You may object to processing based on legitimate interests or request review of past or future personal Live visibility by emailing info@kanjidon.com from your registered address. We will assess the request under Article 21 GDPR and explain the outcome.

5. Why we process data

  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR): account access, cloud synchronization, learning features, purchases, optional community functions you use, battles, and support requested by you.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR): service security, abuse and fraud prevention, fair competition, product improvement, diagnostics, pseudonymous analytics, community operation, Live, aggregated statistics, and essential service communications.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR): marketing communications and other optional processing where we specifically request consent. Device permission and your notification choices also control push delivery.
  • Legal obligations (Article 6(1)(c) GDPR): accounting, tax, lawful authority requests, and obligations that apply to platform safety or illegal content.
  • Legal claims (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR): establishing, exercising, or defending legal rights.

Where we rely on legitimate interests, you may object as described in Section 9.

6. Who receives data

  • Other signed-in Kanjidon users: the Live, profile, leaderboard, showcase, friendship, and battle information described above.
  • Supabase: authentication, database, storage, realtime, and Edge Functions. The primary Kanjidon database is hosted in the EU West (Paris) region.
  • Google and Firebase: Google sign-in, Analytics, Cloud Messaging, Remote Config, Android speech recognition where provided by the device, and related platform services.
  • Apple: Sign in with Apple, App Store distribution, purchases, subscription processing, and Apple speech recognition where provided by the device.
  • Google Play: distribution, purchases, subscriptions, and receipt validation.
  • Brevo: transactional, operational, support, report, and consented marketing email delivery.
  • country.is: transient country lookup from the connection IP address.
  • Authorities and professional advisers: only where legally required or necessary to protect legal rights.

We do not sell personal data or provide it to data brokers. Providers process data under their own terms and, where applicable, data-processing agreements.

7. International transfers

Some providers may process data outside the European Economic Area. Where required, transfers rely on an adequacy decision, the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses, or another lawful transfer mechanism. Provider infrastructure and transfer arrangements may change; current details can be requested from us.

8. Retention

  • Account, profile, and learning data: generally for the life of the account, then deleted or de-identified unless limited retention is required by law, security, backups, or legal claims.
  • Live: normally displayed for 30 days; underlying personal event records may remain for the life of the relevant account as described in Section 4.4.
  • Friendships, blocks, and gameplay: while needed to provide the feature, maintain safety, or resolve disputes.
  • Reports and support: while open and afterwards for the period reasonably necessary for safety, repeat-abuse analysis, accountability, and legal claims.
  • Purchases: for the period required for subscription management, fraud prevention, accounting, tax, and legal obligations.
  • FCM tokens: until replaced, invalidated, no longer needed, or the account is deleted.
  • Shadowing voice samples and transcripts: not retained in Kanjidon's backend; they are used for the current recognition request and exercise. Platform-provider handling is governed by the provider and device settings.
  • Analytics and technical logs: according to the configured provider retention and for no longer than reasonably necessary for measurement, reliability, and security.
  • Marketing: until consent is withdrawn, plus minimal suppression information needed to respect the opt-out.

Deletion from active systems may not immediately remove data from encrypted backups. Backup copies are isolated and expire according to the provider's backup lifecycle.

9. Your rights and choices

Depending on the circumstances, you may request access, rectification, erasure, restriction, or portability of your personal data; object to processing based on legitimate interests; and withdraw consent without affecting earlier lawful processing.

  • Use the in-app profile and settings controls for available profile, online-status, battle-request, notification, block, and account-deletion choices.
  • Email info@kanjidon.com from your registered address for other requests, including a Live objection.
  • Use the unsubscribe link in a marketing email to stop marketing messages.

We may need to verify your identity. We normally respond within one month, subject to the extensions and exceptions allowed by law. Rights are not absolute; if we cannot fulfil a request, we will explain why where the law requires it.

10. User content, reports, and blocks

Display names, deck names, showcases, and other user-provided text must follow the Terms of Service. Kanjidon may restrict content or accounts to protect users, enforce the Terms, or comply with law.

You can block or report another user from that user's profile. A report is sent to Kanjidon for review and may include your verified account email, the reported account, a reason, and an optional description. Your reporter identity is not intentionally disclosed to the reported user, except where required by law.

11. Security

We use measures appropriate to the service and risk, including encrypted network transport, authentication, database row-level security, restricted service roles, access controls, validation, logging, and managed infrastructure safeguards. No online service can guarantee absolute security.

Please protect your device and authentication account and contact us promptly if you suspect unauthorized access.

12. Website storage and external links

The public website may use local browser storage for essential choices such as dismissing the privacy notice. It does not intentionally use profiling cookies on the static marketing pages. Links to third-party services are governed by those services' privacy notices.

13. Changes to this policy

We will update the date and version when this policy changes. If a change materially affects users' rights or introduces processing that requires a new choice, we will provide an appropriate in-app, website, or email notice and request consent where the law requires it.
