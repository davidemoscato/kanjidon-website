Duolingo Alternative for Japanese: Learn Kanji for Real

Duolingo is fantastic for getting started with Japanese. The interface is engaging, lessons are short, and the green owl reminds you to study every day. But after a few weeks, you notice a problem: the kanji just won't stick. And it's not your fault.

The Duolingo kanji problem

Duolingo is designed for general language learning. It works great for Spanish, French, or German, where the alphabet is similar to English. But Japanese is different: it has three writing systems (hiragana, katakana, kanji) and requires memorizing over 2,000 characters just to read a newspaper.
