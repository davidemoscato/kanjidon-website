Free Kanji Apps: The Best in 2026

Want to learn kanji but don't want to spend a fortune on subscriptions? Good news: there are excellent free apps (or nearly free) for studying Japanese characters. Here's an honest comparison of available options.

What to look for in a free app

Not all "free" apps are equal. Some are completely free, others are freemium (free with limits). Here's what to evaluate:

  • How much content is really free? — Just N5 or up to N1?
  • Are there daily limits? — Can you study as much as you want?
  • Are important features free? — SRS, quizzes, offline?
  • Invasive ads? — Ads every 30 seconds?

The best free kanji apps

1. Anki (Desktop + Android)

Cost: Free on desktop and Android, $24.99 on iOS

Type: Fully customizable SRS flashcards

Pros:

  • 100% free on desktop and Android
  • One of the best SRS algorithms
  • Community-shared decks
  • Totally customizable

Cons:

  • Dated, unintuitive interface
  • Requires time to configure
  • No gamification
  • Expensive on iOS ($24.99)

Best for: Technical users who love customizing everything.

2. Duolingo

Cost: Free with ads, Super $7.99/month

Type: General language course with kanji included

Pros:

  • Completely free (with ads)
  • Beautiful, gamified interface
  • Perfect for absolute beginners

Cons:

  • Kanji isn't the focus
  • Limited coverage (~N4)
  • SRS not optimized for kanji
  • Frequent ads in free version

Best for: Those who want to start with Japanese in general.

3. Takoboto

Cost: Free (Android only)

Type: Japanese dictionary with flashcards

Pros:

  • Completely free, no ads
  • Complete offline dictionary
  • Integrated flashcards

Cons:

  • Android only
  • More dictionary than study app
  • Basic SRS
  • No gamification

Best for: Those looking for a free dictionary with flashcards.

4. Kanji Study

Cost: Free (N5), $12.99 to unlock everything

Type: Kanji database with quizzes

Pros:

  • N5 completely free
  • Complete, detailed database
  • Stroke order animations
  • One-time purchase (no subscription)

Cons:

  • Android only
  • Only N5 is free
  • More reference than active study
  • Basic quizzes

Best for: Those who want a detailed kanji reference.

5. Kanjidon

Cost: Free with complete features, optional Premium

Type: Gamified app with SRS and PvP battles

Pros:

  • All basic features free
  • SM-2 algorithm (scientific SRS)
  • 8 quiz types included
  • Complete coverage: kana → N1
  • Free PvP battles
  • 21 languages supported
  • Works offline
  • No ads

Cons:

  • Some advanced features require Premium
  • Relatively new app

Best for: Those who want a complete, modern app without paying.

If you're an absolute beginner: Duolingo is perfect for the first few weeks, then switch to something more specialized.
