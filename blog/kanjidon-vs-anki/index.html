Here's Anki's dirty secret: before you learn a single kanji, you'll spend hours setting up. You need to find a good deck (there are hundreds, most mediocre), configure settings, maybe install add-ons, sync across devices, and figure out the interface. Many learners give up before they start.

Anki decks vary wildly in quality. Some popular kanji decks have errors, outdated information, or poor formatting. You might study for months before realizing your deck teaches incorrect readings. Creating your own deck is an option, but that's dozens of hours of work before you learn anything.
