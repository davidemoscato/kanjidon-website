JLPT N5 Kanji List: 100 Essential Kanji

The JLPT N5 is the first step in your journey to Japanese mastery. It requires knowledge of about 100 kanji. Here's the complete list with meanings and readings, organized by category.

What is JLPT N5?

The Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) is the official Japanese exam recognized worldwide. The N5 level is the basic level, designed for those who:

  • Can read hiragana and katakana
  • Know the most common phrases and expressions
  • Can understand simple conversations

How to study these kanji

Having a list is useful, but not enough. To really memorize N5 kanji you need:

  • Spaced repetition (SRS) — So you don't forget
  • Context — Words and sentences, not isolated kanji
  • Active practice — Quizzes, not just passive reading
  • Consistency — 15-20 minutes every day
