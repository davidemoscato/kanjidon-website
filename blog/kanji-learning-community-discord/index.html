Studying Kanji Alone Is Fine. Studying Together Is Better.

5 min min read

You can absolutely learn kanji alone. Millions have. But there's a reason most of them took years longer than necessary, or gave up entirely. Studying in isolation works until it doesn't. And when motivation drops, there's no one there to catch you.

The Problem With Studying Alone

Day one: excitement. Day seven: still going strong. Day thirty: starting to skip sessions. Day sixty: the app sends you 'we miss you' notifications. Sound familiar?

It's not a discipline problem. It's a structure problem. When you study alone, there's no one to notice if you stop. No one to answer that grammar question at 11pm. No one to celebrate when you finally nail those N4 kanji.

What Changes When You're Not Alone

Something interesting happens when you study with others. You see someone else struggling with the same kanji you found impossible, and suddenly it feels less personal. You see someone who started after you already passing N5, and it lights a fire. You ask a question and get three different explanations until one clicks.

  • Motivation becomes external, not just internal
  • Questions get answered in minutes, not hours of googling
  • Progress becomes visible when you compare with others
  • Bad days feel less lonely
  • Good days get celebrated

The Hard Part: Finding a Good Community

Not all communities are equal. Some Discord servers are ghost towns. Others are so big your message disappears in seconds. Some are 90% memes and 10% actual learning. Finding a place that's active, focused, and welcoming? That's the real challenge.

A good community has people at your level. Has moderators who keep things on track. Has channels organized for different topics. And most importantly: has people who actually respond when you ask something.

The Kanjidon Community

We built a Discord server specifically for Kanjidon users and Japanese learners in general. It's not huge (yet), but it's active. People share their progress, ask questions, and help each other through the kanji grind.

  • Channels for each JLPT level (N5 to N1)
  • Daily study check-ins for accountability
  • Quick answers from fellow learners
  • A place to celebrate milestones
  • No spam, no toxicity, just learning

Join us: https://discord.gg/RnrPqYnC

You Don't Have to Do This Alone

Kanji is a marathon, not a sprint. And marathons are easier when you're running with others. Whether you're on day one or day one thousand, having a community behind you makes the journey less lonely and more sustainable.

Studying alone is fine. But studying together? That's how you actually finish.
